About Warner Bros.

Our Story

Warner Bros. boasts a profound heritage spanning close to a hundred years of influential entertainment. Its inception traces back to 1923 when Harry, Albert, Sam, and Jack Warner – a quartet of determined siblings – established Warner Bros. Studios. These resourceful pioneers left an indelible mark on the film landscape by introducing groundbreaking advancements, notably synchronized sound in The Jazz Singer (1927), marking the advent of sound cinema. This milestone, hailed as the first "talkie," heralded a new era in filmmaking, forever reshaping the industry's trajectory.

Throughout the years, Warner Bros. has nurtured enduring figures such as Bugs Bunny, Batman, Wonder Woman, and Harry Potter. The studio has delivered pioneering cinematic works, ranging from iconic classics like Casablanca to contemporary blockbusters like The Dark Knight trilogy. Today, Warner Bros. remains at the forefront of innovation across various platforms, including film, television, animation, video games, and the ever-expanding realm of interactive entertainment.

Fun Facts

  • Did you know? The iconic Warner Bros. water tower isn't just a set piece It's a functioning water tower that still serves the studio lot.
  • Mel Blanc, the "Man of a Thousand Voices" responsible for voicing Bugs Bunny, Daffy Duck, and countless Looney Tunes characters, is buried in Hollywood Forever Cemetery with the epitaph: "That's All Folks!"
  • Warner Bros. wasn't always "Warner Bros." The brothers' original surname was "Wonskolaser."
